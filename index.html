Future Connect

A mobile app designed for high school students to search and apply for volunteer opportunities to fulfill their community service hours. The app is  accessible to users with colorblindness, featuring carefully chosen colors and a clear hierarchy. It offers educational workshops to raise students' awareness of social issues and the diverse community, promoting long-term participation. This project was shortlisted in the Young Ones ADC Competition in 2023 in the UX/UI category.

Spark! Community Interactive Installation

Transforming the second floor of Boston University’s Data Science building into a creativity hub, this initiative aims to enhance inclusivity and interdisciplinary collaboration. By integrating the College of Fine Arts into the effort, the project introduces a new dynamic to storytelling within the BU Community.
The installation is divided into two main segments. The first segment, a digital signal, significantly contributes to the floor's branding effort. The second segment features a lighting wall, powered by open-source code and driven by a continually evolving series of community-contributed light shows, creating a vibrant and interactive experience for all.

In Search of Feminism and Identity in Asia

 
In Search of Feminism and Identity in Asia is a non-judgmental space dedicated to exploring the nuances of feminism and women’s identity within Asian Gen-Z cultures. It features conversations with women from Asia about themes of independence and culture. Each site offers a discussion — an insight into various views on how these women understand, perceive, and respond to feminism and gender inequality. This project aims to detaboo the term “feminism” in Asian culture and attempts to incorporate the physical space representing intimacy into a digital format, empowering Asians to discuss, share, listen and learn about feminism in trusted relationships.

You’ve Made Me Feel Compassion

This site-based interactive experience sets the scenario of classmates being attacked and observes how participants react to the situation. The experience explores the transformation of an individual’s journey from outrage to action in response to the Xuzhou Chained Woman Incident. It examines society’s tendency to normalize injustices such as sex trafficking, the personal and collective struggle with empathy burnout, and the discovery of compassion as a more sustainable response. Through a blend of personal narrative, research, and interactive elements, visitors are invited to understand the differences between empathy and compassion, and how the latter can inspire positive social change.
